Paris  ·  July 25–30

Five days in the City of Light. The Eiffel Tower at night, Versailles in the afternoon heat, the Louvre before the crowds arrive — and a train north to Amsterdam on the 30th.

⚠Book immediately: Eiffel Tower tickets open 60 days before your visit (late May 2026) and sell out within hours. Louvre timed entry, Versailles, Notre Dame, and the Catacombs also require advance booking. Don't wait.
DAY 1  ·  SAT JUL 25
Overnight Flight — Orlando → Paris
Depart Orlando · arrive Paris CDG Sun Jul 26 at 7:25 AM
  • DEPARTFly out of Orlando International (MCO) on Saturday July 25. Most transatlantic flights from Orlando connect through a hub — confirm your routing and layover. Pack light layers for the flight; overnight transatlantic cabins run cold.
  • IN FLIGHTTry to sleep — you land at 7:25 AM Paris time. The more rest you get now, the better your first full day will be. Download offline maps and your hotel confirmation before boarding in case of data issues on arrival.
  • TIPSet your watch to Paris time (Central European Summer Time, UTC+2) as soon as you board. Mentally commit to staying awake until at least 9 PM Paris time on arrival day — it's the single best way to beat jet lag.
★Pack an eye mask, neck pillow, and noise-cancelling headphones or earplugs. Even a few hours of sleep on the overnight crossing makes a real difference when you land.
DAY 2  ·  SUN JUL 26
Arrival Morning + First Paris Day
Land 7:25 AM · settle in · Eiffel Tower · Musée d'Orsay · Seine cruise
  • 7:25 AMLand at Paris CDG. Follow signs to RER B — it runs directly from Terminal 2 and takes ~45 min to city center (~€12). At any metro station buy a Navigo Découverte weekly pass (~€30 + €5 card) — covers all metro, bus, and RER including CDG all week. Bring a passport photo for the card.
  • 9:30 AMCheck in or drop bags at hotel. Freshen up. Coffee and a croissant at the nearest boulangerie — your first real one. Take 30 minutes before doing anything else.
  • 11:00 AMEiffel Tower summit — pre-booked timed entry. The view from the top mid-morning with clear light over the city is extraordinary. Book at toureiffel.paris (opens ~60 days ahead). Allow 1.5–2 hours.
  • 1:00 PMWalk the Champ de Mars. Lunch in the 7th — Rue Cler market street is ideal for a relaxed, affordable, very Parisian spread.
  • 3:00 PMMusée d'Orsay — the world's greatest Impressionist collection. Monet, Renoir, Van Gogh, Degas, all in a converted railway station. Pre-book at musee-orsay.fr. Allow 2 hours. Closed Mondays.
  • 6:30 PMSeine River cruise at sunset — 1 hour past Notre Dame, the Louvre, Pont Alexandre III, and the Eiffel Tower glowing as dusk falls. Book at bateauxparisiens.com.
  • 8:30 PMDinner in St-Germain. Book a table in advance — Paris in July is packed. Push through to at least 9–10 PM before sleeping. First night jet lag management starts now.
DAY 3  ·  MON JUL 27
The Louvre + Notre Dame + The Marais
  • 9:00 AMLouvre Museum — timed entry, allow 2–3 hours. The Mona Lisa, Venus de Milo, Winged Victory of Samothrace. Book at louvre.fr. Closed Tuesdays — Monday is perfect.
  • 12:30 PMLunch in the Palais Royal garden — a peaceful, beautiful courtyard steps from the Louvre.
  • 2:30 PMNotre Dame Cathedral — recently reopened after restoration. Stunning. Free, but book a timed reservation at notredamedeparis.fr to skip the queue.
  • 4:00 PMSainte-Chapelle — the most beautiful stained glass in the world, bar none. A 13th-century chapel where every wall is glass. Book at sainte-chapelle.fr.
  • 5:30 PMWalk into the Marais — medieval Paris at its best. Place des Vosges (Paris's oldest square), then through the lively Jewish Quarter.
  • 7:00 PML'As du Fallafel, Rue des Rosiers — the most legendary falafel in Paris. Worth the short wait. Then dinner or drinks in the Marais.
★Paris Museum Pass (4-day) covers Louvre, Orsay, Versailles, Arc de Triomphe, Sainte-Chapelle, and 55+ more sites with skip-the-line access. Does NOT cover the Eiffel Tower or Catacombs. Worth it if you're visiting 3+ museums. parismuseumpass.com
DAY 4  ·  TUE JUL 28
Versailles Day Trip
RER C from Gare d'Austerlitz · ~40 min each way
  • 8:00 AMRER C from Musée d'Orsay or Gare d'Austerlitz station → Versailles-Château-Rive Gauche. ~40 minutes, ~€4 each way. Trains run frequently.
  • 9:30 AMPalace of Versailles — Hall of Mirrors, King's and Queen's State Apartments, Royal Chapel. Book skip-the-line at chateauversailles.fr. Allow 2 hours inside. The scale is staggering.
  • 12:00 PMGrand Canal rowboat (~€17/hour) — one of the best experiences in France. Row out into the canal with the palace behind you.
  • 2:00 PMGrand Trianon and Petit Trianon palaces — quieter, equally beautiful. Marie Antoinette's hamlet is nearby.
  • 4:30 PMReturn to Paris by RER C.
  • 7:30 PMMontmartre at sunset — climb to Sacré-Cœur, watch the city go golden below, find a table in the village of Place du Tertre for dinner.
DAY 5  ·  WED JUL 29
Catacombs + Arc de Triomphe + Farewell Paris
  • 9:30 AMParis Catacombs — the underground ossuary holding 6 million Parisians beneath the city. Eerie, fascinating, and completely unlike anything else. Walk-up waits can be 2+ hours in summer. Skip-the-line ticket mandatory. catacombes.paris.fr
  • 11:30 AMArc de Triomphe — climb 284 steps for 360° views over 12 radiating avenues. The Eiffel Tower, the Louvre, all of it from above. Book at paris-arc-de-triomphe.fr.
  • 1:30 PMLunch on the Champs-Élysées or in the 8th. Last proper Paris meal — make it count.
  • 3:30 PMAfternoon free: Galeries Lafayette rooftop (free, stunning view), final souvenir shopping, or simply sit at a café and watch Paris do its thing.
  • 6:00 PMBack to hotel. Pack bags. Set everything up for the morning — train tomorrow at 10:55 AM.
  • 7:30 PMFarewell Paris dinner — book a proper bistro in advance. Chez Janou in the Marais is wonderful. Tomorrow: Amsterdam.
✓Train tomorrow: Gare du Nord, 10:55 AM. Leave hotel by 9:45 AM. The station is large — arrive 20 minutes before departure.

DAY 7  ·  FRI JUL 31
Anne Frank House + Rijksmuseum + Street Parties
  • 9:00 AMAnne Frank House — timed entry, pre-booked months ahead. The secret annex where Anne and her family hid for over two years is preserved exactly as it was. Among the most moving experiences in Europe. Allow 1.5 hours. annefrank.org
  • 11:00 AMDe Negen Straatjes (Nine Streets) — Amsterdam's finest boutique shopping between Prinsengracht and Singel. Unique souvenirs, local designers, beautiful canal-side browsing.
  • 1:00 PMFoodhallen — Amsterdam's beloved indoor food market on Kinkerstraat. Every cuisine, great atmosphere, cold beer.
  • 2:30 PMRijksmuseum — Rembrandt's Night Watch, Vermeer's The Milkmaid, the full sweep of the Dutch Golden Age. Book timed entry at rijksmuseum.nl. Allow 2–3 hours.
  • 6:00 PMVondelpark — relax with a drink from the park café before the evening begins. Amsterdam's most beautiful park in peak summer.
  • 8:00 PMWorldPride Street Parties open across 12+ city locations. Leidseplein, Rembrandtplein, and Reguliersdwarsstraat (the main LGBTQ+ street) are the heart of it. The parade is tomorrow — tonight is the warm-up.
DAY 8  ·  SAT AUG 1 ★
The Canal Parade — Once in a Lifetime
~80 boats · 500,000+ spectators · Parade starts ~1:00 PM
  • 9:00 AMClaim your spot now. Prinsengracht and Keizersgracht fill completely by late morning. Bring sunscreen, snacks, water, and a small folding stool. Very little shade along the canal banks in August.

  • 5:00 PMPost-parade. Street parties and outdoor concerts continue across the city into the night.
  • 7:30 PMDinner — book in advance, every restaurant in the city will be full. Then celebrate as long as you reasonably can.

⚠Flight tomorrow is 7:00 AM — hotel departure is 4:45 AM by pre-booked taxi. Enjoy the parade fully, but give yourself a hard stop tonight. The ship leaves without you if you miss the flight.

Taxi from the terminal to Sagrada Família: ~10–15 min, ~€12–18. Or take the cruise port shuttle bus (€3) to the Columbus Monument at the bottom of Las Ramblas.
10:30 AM–12:30 PM — Sagrada Família
Gaudí's unfinished masterpiece — the most visited monument in Spain, and one of the most astonishing buildings on earth. The nave is filled with colored light through Art Nouveau stained glass in every direction. Book a morning timed entry at sagradafamilia.org. Allow 1.5–2 hours. This is the non-negotiable Barcelona experience.
12:45 PM — Tapas lunch
Gothic Quarter or El Born neighborhood. Pan con tomate, jamón ibérico, croquetas, patatas bravas, a cold cava. This is your one meal in Barcelona — make it excellent. Walk two streets back from Las Ramblas for real food at real prices.
2:00 PM — Waterfront or Gothic Quarter
Ten minutes from the Gothic Quarter: the Mediterranean. Walk the Barceloneta promenade. Visit the free Barcelona Cathedral. Wander the medieval lanes of the Barri Gòtic. Keep it relaxed — you've already had a big morning.
3:00 PM — Head back to the terminal
Taxi from the Gothic Quarter / Columbus Monument to Moll Adossat: ~10 min, ~€12. Or the cruise shuttle bus from the Columbus Monument: €3, ~15 min. Leave by 3:00 PM to arrive with comfortable buffer before the 4:30 PM all-aboard.
3:30 PM — Check in and board
If you completed Royal Caribbean online check-in in advance (royalcaribbean.com → My Cruises — do this before you leave Amsterdam), you go straight to the boarding lane. Takes 10 minutes online, saves 30 at the terminal.
~4:00 PM — Onboard Brilliance of the Seas
Stateroom ready. Drop your daypack and go straight to Guest Services. Book immediately: (1) Ravenna → Venice shuttle (~$58/person) and (2) specialty restaurant reservations. Both fill within the first day. Then explore the ship.
4:30 PM — ALL ABOARD · Gangway closes
This is your hard deadline. Every passenger must be back on the ship by 4:30 PM. The ship does not wait.
5:00 PM — BRILLIANCE OF THE SEAS DEPARTS BARCELONA
Top deck or stern. Drink in hand. Watch Sagrada Família's towers and Barcelona's skyline shrink behind you as the ship heads out into the Mediterranean. The cruise has begun. 🥂
✓The timing is genuinely comfortable. 5.5 hours between landing and boarding cutoff, airport-to-port in 20–30 minutes, real buffer for any delays. The only things that need to be locked in advance: Sagrada Família timed entry + RC online check-in.

Brilliance of the Seas  ·  August 3–10

Seven nights aboard Royal Caribbean's Brilliance of the Seas — Barcelona to Ravenna via Toulon, Corsica, Rome, Naples, and Sicily. Note: the cruise ends in Ravenna, not Venice. Transfer to Venice takes 2.5–3 hours. Book the RC shuttle at Guest Services on Day 1.

⚠Ravenna ≠ Venice. Royal Caribbean markets this as "Venice (Ravenna)" but Ravenna is 90 miles from Venice. Book the RC shuttle to Venice Piazzale Roma (~$58/person) at Guest Services on your first day aboard. It will sell out.

Day 5 · Friday August 7 — Naples, Italy
GANGWAY OPEN 7:30 AM – ALL ABOARD 6:30 PM  ·  11 HOURS ASHORE

11 full hours in one of Italy's most intense and delicious cities — with Pompeii and the Amalfi Coast right outside the door. The port is in the heart of Naples, steps from the city center. Three world-class options for the day.

Option A — Pompeii (recommended): The UNESCO-listed ancient Roman city buried by Vesuvius in 79 AD. 163 acres of preserved streets, homes, baths, frescoes, and plaster casts of victims. One of the most extraordinary archaeological sites on earth. Take Alibus from port → Naples Centrale (~20 min), then Circumvesuviana train → Pompeii Scavi (~30 min). Entrance ~€18. Allow 3–4 hours inside. Tickets now limited to 20,000/day — book in advance at pompeii.beniculturali.it. Plan to be heading back by 4:30 PM to make all-aboard at 6:30 PM comfortably.

Option B — Amalfi Coast: One of the world's most dramatic coastlines — Positano, Ravello, Amalfi town. Best done via RC shore excursion or private car. The hairpin roads are not suitable for independent driving.

Option C — Naples itself: Piazza del Plebiscito, Castel Nuovo, the underground catacombs, Spaccanapoli — and the birthplace of pizza at L'Antica Pizzeria da Michele (since 1870).

Day 6 · Saturday August 8 — Messina, Sicily
GANGWAY OPEN 8:30 AM – ALL ABOARD 4:30 PM  ·  8 HOURS ASHORE

Messina sits at the northeastern tip of Sicily, two miles across the strait from mainland Italy. The port is walkable to the center.

In Messina: The famous Astronomical Clock on the Cathedral belltower puts on a spectacular 12-minute mechanical show at noon every day — do not miss this. The Messina Cathedral itself (dome from 1551), Piazza Cairoli for gelato and espresso, and the 12th-century Church of Santissima Annunziata dei Catalani — a rare example of Norman-Arab-Byzantine architecture.

Day trip to Taormina (highly recommended): A 45-minute bus ride brings you to a clifftop medieval town with an ancient Greek theater overlooking snow-capped Mount Etna and the Ionian Sea. One of the most beautiful views in Europe. Corso Umberto I for shopping, Piazza IX Aprile terrace for the view, Teatro Greco for the history. Allow 2.5–3 hours in Taormina. Leave Taormina by 2:30 PM to catch the bus back (~45 min) and make the 4:30 PM all-aboard with comfortable margin.

Day 8 · Monday August 10 — Ravenna, Italy
ARRIVES 3:30 AM  ·  DISEMBARK FROM ~7:00 AM

The ship arrives at Ravenna in the small hours. Disembarkation is organized by deck group — if you set out bags the night before, you'll be called early. Self-carry guests depart from ~7:00 AM.

Ravenna → Venice options:

Option 1 (recommended): RC Coach Transfer to Venice Piazzale Roma (~$58/person, ~2.5 hrs). Book at Guest Services on Day 1. From Piazzale Roma, Vaporetto Line 1 or 2 takes you into Venice in 25 minutes (~€9.50).

Option 2: RC shuttle to Ravenna train station ($10/person, 20 min), then train to Venice Santa Lucia via Ferrara or Bologna (~3 hrs, ~$25). More flexible if you want time in Ravenna first.

Option 3: Pre-booked private transfer through Viator or Pantarei Chauffeur Service — door to door, ~€120–160 for a group, ~2.5 hours.

⚠Ravenna is 90 miles from Venice. Budget the full 2.5–3 hours of travel time. Don't book a lunchtime reservation in Venice until you know your transfer is confirmed and you understand the arrival time.
Venice  ·  August 10–12

La Serenissima — the Most Serene Republic, built on 118 islands connected by 400 bridges and 150 canals. After a week at sea, arriving in Venice feels like the trip's reward. Two full days, and not nearly enough.

TRANSFER DAY  ·  MON AUG 10
Ravenna → Venice (2.5–3 hrs)
  • 7:00 AMDisembark. Collect luggage in the Ravenna terminal. Board your pre-booked RC shuttle or private transfer.
  • 9:30–10:00 AMArrive Venice Piazzale Roma — the mainland gateway. No motor vehicles past this point. This is where the city truly begins.
  • 10:00 AMStore luggage: your hotel (most allow early bag drop even if rooms aren't ready), or Santa Lucia train station left luggage (~€8/bag). Don't drag rolling bags over cobblestones longer than necessary.
  • 10:30 AMTake Vaporetto Line 1 down the Grand Canal to San Marco (~25 min, ~€9.50). The Grand Canal itself — flanked by centuries of palaces — is one of the great journeys in travel.
  • 12:00 PMFirst look at Piazza San Marco. Lunch nearby — avoid eating directly in the Piazza (tourist pricing). Walk two streets back from the square for better food at half the price.
ℹBuy a 48-hr or 72-hr Vaporetto pass (~€35–45) at Piazzale Roma or Santa Lucia station. You'll use it constantly — for the Grand Canal, islands, and all movement around the city. Worth every cent.
MON AUG 10 — AFTERNOON
St. Mark's Basilica + Doge's Palace + Gondola at Sunset
  • 1:30 PMSt. Mark's Basilica — free entry but book a timed slot at prenotazionib.venetonet.it to skip the long queue. The golden Byzantine mosaics covering every ceiling and wall are extraordinary. The rooftop terrace gives a view directly over the Piazza.
  • 3:00 PMDoge's Palace — the seat of Venetian power for 700 years. Tintoretto's "Paradise" (the world's largest oil painting), the Bridge of Sighs, the prison cells below. Book at palazzoducale.visitmuve.it. Allow 1.5–2 hours.
  • 5:30 PMAperitivo hour in Dorsoduro or Cannaregio — find a bacaro (Venetian wine bar), order cicchetti (small bites) and a Spritz Aperol. This is the authentic Venetian experience most tourists miss entirely.
  • 7:00 PMGondola ride at sunset — negotiate at any gondola stand near San Marco or Rialto. Fixed rate: ~€80 for 30 min. Go through the small interior canals, not the Grand Canal itself — quieter, more beautiful, more intimate. The light at golden hour on old stone and water is unforgettable.
  • 8:30 PMDinner in Cannaregio — the least touristy of Venice's six sestieri. Try Osteria Boccadoro or any of the restaurants along the northern canals. Book in advance.
DAY  ·  TUE AUG 11
Rialto Market + Accademia + Murano + Burano
  • 7:30 AMRialto Market — Venice's historic fish and produce market, best visited early before the day-trippers arrive. The Rialto Bridge at 7:30 AM with almost no one else on it is a rare and beautiful thing.
  • 9:30 AMGallerie dell'Accademia — the greatest collection of Venetian painting in the world. Bellini, Carpaccio, Titian, Veronese, Tiepolo. Pre-book at gallerieaccademia.it. Allow 1.5–2 hours.
  • 12:00 PMPeggy Guggenheim Collection (next door to Accademia) — world-class modern art in a stunning palazzo on the Grand Canal. Picasso, Dalí, Pollock, Calder. Optional but outstanding.
  • 1:30 PMLunch in Dorsoduro — the student neighborhood. More authentic and affordable than near San Marco. The Zattere waterfront is beautiful for eating outside.
  • 3:00 PMMurano — Vaporetto Line 4.1 from Fondamente Nove, ~15 minutes. Famous for centuries of glassblowing. Watch a live demonstration at any of the traditional fornaci (furnaces). Buy directly from artisans for the best quality and authentic pieces.
  • 4:30 PMBurano — ferry from Murano, ~35 min. The absurdly colorful fishing village of the Venetian lagoon — every house painted a different vivid color. Lacemaking tradition, quiet canals, the best views in the lagoon. Return ferry to Venice.
  • 7:30 PMFinal dinner in Venice — treat yourself. Try Trattoria alla Madonna (classic since 1954) or Al Covo in Castello. Venice dining is expensive but worth doing once properly.
  • 9:30 PMEvening walk across Venice — cross the Rialto, end at Piazza San Marco after dark. The reflection of lights on water, the sound of nothing but boats and quiet voices. This is why you came.
⚠Venice's cobblestones are brutal on rolling luggage. Store bags at the hotel and use a daypack. Wear flat, closed-toe shoes — you will walk miles on uneven stone every single day. Flip-flops will destroy your feet.
DAY  ·  WED AUG 12
Departure Day
  • MORNINGFinal espresso at a canal-side café. Pick up last gifts — Murano glass near Rialto, Carnevale masks in the Dorsoduro shops, Italian leather goods, olive oil, limoncello.
  • DEPARTVenice Marco Polo Airport (VCE): Vaporetto Line 5 or ATVO bus from Piazzale Roma → airport (~30 min). Or a water taxi from any dock (~€120 — expensive but completely unforgettable as a final memory). Allow 2 hours before your flight.
✓You did it. Arrivederci.
